Masterclass threat intelligence Howest

Howest recently hosted an event featuring Niels Desloovere, who shared his expertise on critical cybersecurity topics. The event emphasized the importance of threat intelligence in protecting against evolving cyber threats. Desloovere highlighted the efforts of Cyber Crime Belgium (CCB) in combating cybercrime and fostering collaboration between public and private sectors. He also discussed the MITRE ATT&CK framework, explaining its role in understanding and defending against adversary tactics and techniques. A key part of the event was a live demonstration of MISP (Malware Information Sharing Platform & Threat Sharing). Desloovere showed how MISP helps organizations share structured threat information to enhance detection, analysis, and response capabilities. The session concluded with an engaging Q&A, providing attendees with practical insights and reinforcing the importance of continuous learning and collaboration in cybersecurity. Howest's event was a valuable opportunity for participants to stay updated on the latest trends and tools in the field, underscoring the critical role of threat intelligence and information sharing.
