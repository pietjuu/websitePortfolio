Raspberry Pi projects

These Raspberry Pi scripts mark my hands-on journey into the realm of cybersecurity, showcasing the fusion of coding with tangible hardware experiences. In the initial script, PushButtonLed, I explored the basics of connecting a button and LED to my Raspberry Pi, unraveling the foundational principles of hardware interaction through code. It laid the groundwork for understanding how simple components could be manipulated programmatically.

As the exploration progressed into PushButtonLedServoMotor, the complexity increased. This script orchestrates a sequence of events triggered by a button press—lights turning on and a series of motors activating at intervals. It's like a small-scale simulation, providing a glimpse into how a Raspberry Pi could control multiple components, akin to a security setup. This not only strengthened my coding skills but also deepened my appreciation for the real-world applications of such setups.

The StopServo script introduces the concept of control and intervention. Acting like an emergency brake, it swiftly stops the motors, ensuring a sense of order and preventing unexpected outcomes. This experience instilled valuable lessons in managing and controlling ongoing actions within my projects, a crucial aspect in any cybersecurity endeavor.

Concluding with StartServo, I've begun envisioning a broader project—a Raspberry Pi orchestrating a small vehicle. These scripts serve as the initial building blocks of a practical exploration where cybersecurity principles seamlessly integrate with physical hardware, unveiling the exciting potential where code translates into tangible actions.
