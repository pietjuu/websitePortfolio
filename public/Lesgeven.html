Teaching Cybersecurity: A Lesson for High School Students

A while ago, I had the opportunity to teach a lesson on cybersecurity to high school students. The goal was to share my knowledge and raise awareness about the importance of cybersecurity and how easily systems can be exploited if not properly secured. During the lesson, I covered several key topics. First, I explained basic cybersecurity concepts and the various types of vulnerabilities that can exist in software and hardware. I emphasized how even seemingly small weaknesses can be exploited by malicious actors to gain unauthorized access to systems and data. To make the lesson more engaging, I highlighted how much information can be found online, often more than people realize. This part of the lesson aimed to make the students aware of their digital footprint and the importance of protecting their personal information. The highlight of the session was a live demonstration of the EternalBlue vulnerability. EternalBlue is a well-known exploit that takes advantage of a flaw in the SMB protocol on Windows operating systems. I walked the students through how the exploit works and demonstrated its potential impact by showing how it could be used to gain control of a vulnerable system. This demo was a powerful way to illustrate the real-world implications of cybersecurity threats. The students were highly engaged throughout the lesson, asking insightful questions and expressing a keen interest in learning more about how to protect themselves and their systems from cyber threats. By the end of the session, they had a better understanding of the importance of cybersecurity and the steps they could take to safeguard their digital lives.
