Mars project

As part of our Analysis and Development Project course, involving multiple instructors, the task was to envision Mars as a habitable place and establish a company on the red planet. Our venture had specific criteria, emphasizing community engagement, a visual representation (like a website), user data collection, and fostering user participation.

Thus, Pieter Verheye, Wiebe Desmadryl, Glenn Callens, and Thibo Verbeerst united to birth Shippert. Our company's core concept revolves around a pioneering transportation method using a pipeline and transporter system. The innovation lies in transforming items into molecules, sending them via a pipeline to a receiving transporter, and reconstructing the original item at the destination.

To successfully complete the course requirements, we aimed for a comprehensive set of deliverables: a website, a detailed business case, server-client infrastructure, and an additional implementation of an Internet of Things (IoT) device.

My primary focus was on the IoT device, where I delved into creating a weightscale with a screen using a Raspberry Pi. Additionally, I contributed to the development of the business case, ensuring our project met the outlined objectives and criteria.
