Final assessment high school

For my final assessment in high school, I had the incredible opportunity to participate in the BERT Project, a prestigious competition between schools that presents students with a unique problem to solve each year. The challenge for our year was to detect graffiti on trains and develop an efficient method to remove it.

Together with another student, we approached the problem with innovative solutions. In collaboration with FLIR, we developed a camera equipped with AI capabilities to detect graffiti accurately. This advanced technology allowed for real-time identification of graffiti on train surfaces.

To address the removal process, we designed a lifting hoist that could be easily attached to the trains using suction cups. This mechanism ensured that the cleaning equipment could be securely and swiftly positioned.

Moreover, in partnership with BASF, we formulated an environmentally friendly product specifically designed to remove graffiti. This solution was not only effective in cleaning but also aligned with sustainability principles.

Our project culminated in a presentation attended by approximately 100 people, including political figures and industry experts. The positive reception and interest from the audience were overwhelming. Ultimately, our hard work and innovative approach earned us the public prize, a testament to the effectiveness and creativity of our solutions.

Winning the public prize at the BERT Project competition was a proud moment for us. It demonstrated the power of collaboration, innovation, and dedication in tackling real-world problems. This experience has been a significant highlight of my high school journey, providing valuable lessons and inspiration for future endeavors.
