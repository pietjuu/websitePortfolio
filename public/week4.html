Erasmus week 4 - day 1

Erasmus week 4 - day 2

Today we began planning our upcoming trip to Stockholm, which is scheduled for September 21st. We focused on accommodations and transport options. Considering the train journey can take about four hours, depending on which train we choose.

Although it was a relatively quiet day, none of us felt like cooking, so we decided to eat dinner at an all-you-can-eat restaurant. It was a nice break from our daily routines and a chance to indulge a bit.

The evening brought an exciting event: a big football match between Germany and France. With a lot of German and French students in our dorm, the game was a major event here. Germany clinched the win with a score of 2-1. After the game we went all to sleep!

Erasmus week 4 - day 3

Today marked the first instance of a "cultural clash" among the students, which proved to be quite intense. Some of the Mexican students found themselves in disagreements with students of other nationalities. These moments are often challenging but can serve as learning opportunities for everyone involved to understand and appreciate diverse perspectives better. Other than that, it was a relatively uneventful day without any other notable incidents.

Erasmus week 4 - day 4

Today was a quiet rest day, with everyone focusing on the university. We were all kept busy with homework and other university tasks, taking the time to catch up on their studies and prepare for upcoming classes and assignments.

Erasmus week 4 - day 5

Today was packed with lectures and assignments for university, keeping me busy from morning till evening. Fortunately, after all the hard work, we had a chance to unwind and party at KB. For the first time, there was plenty of tequila available, which meant that pretty much everyone ended up quite drunk.
